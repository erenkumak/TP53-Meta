An interactive *Gost plot* which shows functional enrichment results from several data sources as a Manhattan plot can be created for the filtered gene set. Biological categories that are enriched in the differentially expressed gene set of interest are shown as circles with their p-values on the y-axis and the functional term they are involved in on the x-axis. Each functional term is positioned and colour coded.
<br><br>**Example**
<br>The most relevant GO biological process that is enriched in the previously filtered gene set is seen to be cell communication and among the most relevant GO molecular functions is signaling receptor binding as expected upon the hormone treatments.

The *Gene-Concept Network plot* displays the relation of GO terms and KEGG pathways with linkages of genes within the gene set of interest. It is also possible to visualize the linked biological concepts as a *Heatplot*. The minimum GSSize can be adjusted according to the size of the gene set of interest. The GSSize can be increased for long gene lists to increase the accuracy of the network.
<br><br>Note that one of the initially selected experimental groups should be chosen for the logFC values to use. 
<br><br>**Example**
<br>The network of the 637 selected genes is plotted based on the 2nd data (DRSP+E2 treatment within the MCF7 experiment) with a minimum GSSize of 8.

A *GO Enrichment Analysis* can be done to obtain a comprehensive table of enriched molecular functions (MF), biological processes (BP) and cellular components (CC) based on the differentially expressed genes of interest. The gene counts associated with certain gene ontology terms will be provided with their gene IDs, ratios to the whole sample size, and p-values.
<br><br>**Example**
<br>Cell adhesion and epithelial cell proliferation seem to be among the most impacted biological processes in which several genes such as *CITED2*, *DAB2*, *FUT7* , *DLL4* and *PGR* can be investigated for further analyses.
<br><br>The enriched GO terms can be visualized as a dotplot that enables a straightforward scoring, as a map that enables an organized view taking overlapping genes into account, and as a network plot with the desired layout.
